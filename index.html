About our Alley

FunTime Bowl

In 2008, we realized a long-held dream and purchased the local bowling alley in Lewisburg, TN, the Fun Time Bowling Center. After several years of operations, we discovered integrating the range of skills needed to manage the business aspect of our dream proved to be both complex and nuanced. In early 2016, we took the advice of a local government official and sought the counsel of the Tennessee Small Business Development Center (TSBDC) for guidance in improving the performance of our business.

A TSBDC Business Development Specialist visited us at the Fun Time Bowling Center and guided us through the process of performing both an operations analysis and a financial analysis of the facility. The site visit and financial review identified critical deficiencies in the maintenance of the building and equipment, financial oversight, and marketing strategy. We and the TSBDC Specialist used this analysis to develop an improvement plan for the bowling center.

We enthusiastically embraced the improvement plan. Within a few months, the facilities’ infrastructure was repaired, financial oversight was implemented, and the marketing campaign was executed. The results from the Turner’s execution of the improvement plan were outstanding. In less than a year, revenues increased 62% and profitability increased in excess of 300%.

Working with the TSBDC taught us how to restructure our business from the ground up. They came in as a mentor and gave us a template to stop the downward spiral. Working with the TSBDC also gave us the solid foundation to make changes in our business that gave us a positive outlook on our future. We are so thankful for all their help and input.

  • History of Funtime Bowl

  • September 1960

    W.R. (Spot) Lowe, Tommy Cathey, Dr. James Limbaugh, Dr. Hoyt Harris, and Ed Beatty opened Kegler's Kove Bowling Lanes on Saturday, August 20th and offered a free game of bowling to everyone who came. Then, on Saturday, September 24th, they hosted a Grand Opening along with Pierce Furniture Sales and West End Sundries, who occupied the adjacent businesses.

  • August 1964

    August 3rd: A 15-year old boy named Steve Wallace from Houston, Texas through the first ever 300 game at Kegler's Kove Bowling Lanes. He was in Lewisburg visiting his aunts Mrs. Reavis Coggins, Mrs. Collier Hunter, and Mrs. Orville Parr and their families. He was awarded a new bowling ball for the feat. Steve had won a handsome amount of bowling trophins in the Houston area but also was a very gifted pianist and organist. At 19 years old, he became a member of the Professional Bowlers Association.

    August 20th: James (Wibby) Crunk leases the bowling alley and takes over the management of Kegler's Kove Bowling Lanes on August 30th. James Crunk was an employee at the bowling alley and also connected to Bunch Walgreens Agency Drugs, which was later H&S #2.

  • November 1965

    John R. Lunn, a Home Beneficial Insurance Representative, and Jimmy Harbor, grocery owner and operator, purchased Kegler's Kove Lanes.

  • September 1972

    John R. Lunn leased Kegler's Kove Bowling Lanes to Al and Bernice Deuermeyer of Tullahoma, Tennessee along with 3 of their 5 children: Ronnie, Wayne, and Larry. The alley was managed by Larry Deuermeyer, an experienced bowling alley manager and lane keeper. Before running Kegler's Kove Bowling Lanes, he worked at Tullahoma Lanes.

  • May 1976

    John R. Lunn updated Kegler's Kove Bowling Lanes with the latest pin spotters and ball returns.

  • February 1985

    Kegler's Kove was taken over by Lewisburg Recreation Center and operated under Lewisburg Bowling Center.

  • June 1995

    Bruce and Ellen Gardner purchased the bowling alley under the name of Lewisburg Lanes. Bruce was originally from Florida and had 20 years of experience working in a bowling center. The Gardners found their way to Savannah, Georgia where Bruce worked at a 50 lane bowling center and became the head mechanic.

    Once the Gardners took ownership of the bowling alley, they began a complete overhaul. They started by restoring the big bowling pin sign hanging in front of the bulding with neon lighing. In addition, they repaved the parking lot and striped the parking spots. On the inside, they updated to brighter fluorescent lighting, replaced the old seats, score projectors, the masking units, and new foul lights were added. The biggest upgrades were the new lanes and the addition of the automatic scoring system.

  • April 1996

    Bumpers were added to 6 lanes to make it easier for the young up and coming players to make a strike.

  • August 2002

    The Gardners added bumbers to the remaining lanes, making all 12 lanes equipped for youth bowlers to run up their scores.

  • July 2003

    The Gardners closed the bowling alley in order to complete the rennovation of new HPL Alliance Lanes, high-pressure synthetic lanes that help the ball go faster.

  • 2006

    Bruce and Ellen Gardner leased Lewisburg Lanes to Randy Morey.

  • September 2007

    Bruce and Ellen Gardner returned as owners and changed the name of Lewisburg Lanes to Funtime Bowl. They appointed Chris Fagan as the manager of the alley.

  • 2018

    Bruce and Ellen Gardner sold Funtime Bowl to Michael (Kentucky) and Kim Turner and moved back to Georgia to start their journey again with another bowling center.

  • 2020

    The Turners leased Funtime Bowl to Chris Udell.

  • Septempber 2024

    The Turners took back over Funtime Bowl and continue to improve the bowling center and make it fun and affordable for all ages.
